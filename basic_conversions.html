Basic Lab Conversions

Quick conversions commonly used in optics, RF electronics, and cold-atom experiments. All calculations run locally in your browser.

dBm ↔ power

Convert RF or optical power between dBm, mW, and W.

Result
—
—
P(mW) = 10^(dBm/10)

dBm ↔ voltage

Convert RF power to RMS voltage for a chosen impedance.

Result
—
—
V_rms = sqrt(P · R)

frequency ↔ wavelength

Convert between optical frequency and vacuum wavelength.

Result
—
—
λ = c / f, using c = 299792458 m/s

energy units

Convert frequency to photon energy and temperature equivalent.

Result
—
—
E = h f = k_B T

beam area and intensity

Useful constants

Values used in the conversions above.

Speed of light c = 299792458 m/s
Planck constant h = 6.62607015 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
Boltzmann constant kB = 1.380649 × 10⁻²³ J/K
